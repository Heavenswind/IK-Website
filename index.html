Inverse Kinematics for Arm Tracking.

This project was developped with the intent to be used as an arm tracking inverse kinematics solution in real time without the need for extra sensors to track the full arm.

About FABRIK

Forward and backwards reaching inverse kinematics is a fast iterative solver algorithm developped by Andreas Aristidou to solve the inverse Kinematics problem.

Motivation. An Immersive world.

While playing VR, we would like to give a solution for programmers to be able to implement an IK solution in real-time without the need for extra trackers for their arm in Virtual Reality games.

Echo Arena VR - Source: Google images

Background reading

To be able to find a solution in real time, we looked at various algorithms who solve the IK problem both analytically (Jacobian Inverse) and heuristically (Cycle Coordinate Descent, target triangle and FABRIK). All of the above looked promising but after carefully weighting our pros and cons in terms of performance, we decided to go with the most accurate and fast algorithm: FABRIK

Inverse Kinematics Arm - Source: Google images

Early Implementation. Hello World!

As a first hands-on with the FABRIK algorithm we decided to render a two-bone rig made up of three joints and two bones. With this we would be able to simulate the motion of an arm. After implementing the algorithm and making our target as a function of y=sin(x) and incrementing x +/- 0.1 We got our first taste of the algorithm in action.
